Name
Mexican Snowball

Origin
Native to the semi-desert habitats in Mexico.

How to Grow and Care
Mexican Snowball is certainly one of the most versatile neat, low-growing ground cover succulents. It is easy to care for and is a good starting point for beginning gardeners. It is considered to be drought tolerant but it does best with occasional watering in a well drained soil. This is typical of many Echeveria. They can be planted in full sun in coastal areas and they like some light shade when planted inland. Elsewhere grow as a houseplant in winter in a cool, bright window and keep dry until spring.

Watering
Water moderately when in growth; not at all when dormant.


Name
Haworthia

Origin
Native to Southern Africa.

How to Grow and Care
Bright light, but not direct sunlight. Warmer summers but cool in the winter (down to 50 degrees F).

Watering
Water evenly and generously in the summer, letting the soil media dry out between waterings.

Name
Wisteria

Origin
Native to China, Korea and Japan.

How to Grow and Care
Wisteria likes full sun. Plant in the spring or fall. Wisteria will grow in most soils.

Watering
During the first year wisterias will benefit from regular watering while the roots establish.


Name
Peonies

Origin
Native to Asia, Europe and Western North America.

How to Grow and Care
Peonies likes full or part sun. Plant in the fall, late September and October. Peonies grow best in humus-rich soil that drains well.

Watering
During the first year peonies will benefit from regular watering while the roots establish.


Name
Cherry Blossom

Origin
Native to China and Japan.

How to Grow and Care
Cherry blossom trees like full sun to partial shade. Plant in the spring after the last frost. Cherry trees can adapt to several soil types, but do better in acidic environments.

Watering
During the first year cherry blosoms will benefit from regular watering, but make sure the soil drains well. After the tree is established only water during droughts.
